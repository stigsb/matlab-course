**lesson.md** -- Written lesson covering:
1. Logarithms (MATH-07): Start VISUAL. "How many times do you double 1 to get 8? Three times. That's log2(8) = 3." Analogy: "Logarithms are the 'undo' button for exponents, like subtraction undoes addition." Show the log curve. Key insight: "Doubling the possibilities adds just 1 bit." Cover log2, log, log10 in Matlab. Notation bridge: log_b(x) notation, change of base.
2. Entropy (INFO-01): "Entropy measures surprise. If you already know what's going to happen, there's no surprise (low entropy). If anything could happen, maximum surprise (high entropy)." Analogy: "Predicting the weather in the Sahara (boring, low entropy) vs London (exciting, high entropy)." Fair coin = 1 bit. Formula: H = -sum(p * log2(p)). The 0*log(0) convention. Notation bridge: H(X) = -sum p(x) log2 p(x).
3. Mutual Information (INFO-02): "How much does knowing the neural response reduce your uncertainty about the stimulus?" Build from entropy: MI = H(response) - H(response|stimulus). Analogy: "If a neuron fires a lot, and you know that means 'stimulus on the right,' the neuron's firing gave you information." Joint distribution, conditional entropy, then MI. Notation bridge: I(X;Y) = H(Y) - H(Y|X).
4. Visualizing Information (INFO-03): Venn diagram of H(X), H(Y), H(X,Y), MI(X;Y). Bar charts comparing entropy. Stimulus-response matrices.

Follow established pattern: visual-first, small chunks, notation bridges after visual intuition. CRITICAL: logarithms BEFORE entropy, entropy BEFORE mutual information.

- fprintf: H(R), H(R|S), MI values for both real and shuffled cases
- Plots a conceptual Venn diagram of H(X), H(Y), MI using rectangle/circle annotations
