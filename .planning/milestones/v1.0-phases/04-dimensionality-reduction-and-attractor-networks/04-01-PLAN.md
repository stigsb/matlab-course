**lesson.md** -- Written lesson in 4 parts following established pattern:
- Part 1: The Curse of Dimensions -- Why we need dimensionality reduction (50 neurons recorded, can't plot 50D, most variance in a few directions). Analogy: "finding the best camera angle for a 3D sculpture."
- Part 2: PCA Step-by-Step -- Mean-center, compute covariance with cov(), eigendecompose with eig(), sort descending, project with matrix multiplication. Make the Phase 2 eigenvalue connection EXPLICIT: "Remember eigenvectors from Module 04? They're back -- this time finding the most important directions in your data."
- Part 3: Interpreting PCA -- Scree plots, cumulative variance explained, choosing how many components to keep ("how many dimensions capture 90% of the information?"). Notation bridge from visual understanding to textbook notation.
- Part 4: PCA on Neural Data -- Apply to simulated population recordings. Show that circular stimuli map to rings in PC space.

**demo_why_reduce.m** -- Motivation demo:
- rng(42) for reproducibility
- Generate 50-neuron simulated data where most neurons are driven by 2-3 common signals plus noise (use common_signal approach from research)
- Show: "We have 50 columns of data. We can't visualize 50 dimensions."
- Show 3D scatter of first 3 neurons (this is all we can plot)
- Run quick PCA to show that top 2-3 components capture most variance
- Print variance explained percentages
- Subplot: original 3-neuron view vs PCA 2D projection (colored by time)

**demo_pca_steps.m** -- Step-by-step PCA:
- rng(42), generate 200 points in 3D where data is really 2D (ellipsoid flattened in one direction) using the spiral example from research (2*cos(t), 1.5*sin(t), 0.2*noise)
- Explicitly labeled Step 1-6 with comments and fprintf output:
  - Step 1: "Start with data (rows=observations, columns=variables)"
  - Step 2: "Mean-center" -- mu = mean(data); centered = data - mu;
  - Step 3: "Covariance matrix" -- C = cov(centered); disp(C)
  - Step 4: "Eigendecompose" -- [V, D] = eig(C);
  - Step 5: "Sort largest first" -- [eigenvalues, idx] = sort(diag(D), 'descend'); V = V(:, idx);
  - Step 6: "Project onto top 2 PCs" -- projected = centered * V(:, 1:2);
- Print eigenvalues and variance explained percentages
- Figure 1: subplot(1,2,1) scatter3 original data colored by t, subplot(1,2,2) scatter projected 2D colored by t
- Figure 2: subplot(1,2,1) scree plot (bar chart of eigenvalues), subplot(1,2,2) cumulative variance explained with 90% threshold line

IMPORTANT pitfalls to handle:
- eig() returns ascending order -- MUST sort descending
- Mean-center BOTH for cov() and for projection
- Data convention: rows=observations, columns=variables

**demo_pca_neural.m** -- PCA on neural data:
- rng(42), simulate 8 neurons with circular preferred directions (0 to 315 deg)
- Generate 200 trials with random stimulus directions, cosine tuning + noise (use cosd, max(0,...) for non-negative rates)
- Perform manual PCA (same steps as demo_pca_steps.m)
- Print variance explained
- Figure: scatter of PC1 vs PC2 colored by stimulus direction (should form a ring/circle in PC space!)
- Add annotation: "The circular stimulus maps to a ring in PC space!"
